Class 9 | Ch8: Quadrilaterals | Exercise 8.2

Mid-point Theorem: Line segment joining midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to third side and equals half of it.

Q1. ABCD is a quadrilateral. AC and BD are diagonals. Prove sum of all sides > sum of diagonals, and < 2(sum of diagonals).
Answer: In ‚Ė≥ABC: AB+BC>AC. In ‚Ė≥ACD: CD+DA>AC. In ‚Ė≥ABD: AB+AD>BD. In ‚Ė≥BCD: BC+CD>BD. Adding first two: AB+BC+CD+DA>2AC. Adding last two: AB+BC+CD+DA>2BD. So AB+BC+CD+DA>AC+BD. ‚úÖ For upper bound: AC+BD>AB, AC+BD>BC, etc. ‚Üí 2(AC+BD)>AB+BC+CD+DA. ‚úÖ

Q5. In a parallelogram ABCD, E and F are midpoints of AB and CD. Show that EF is parallel to and bisects the segment AF.
Answer: ABCD parallelogram: AB‚ą•CD and AB=CD. E midpoint AB ‚Üí AE=¬ĹAB. F midpoint CD ‚Üí CF=¬ĹCD. AE=CF and AE‚ą•CF ‚Üí AECF is parallelogram ‚Üí AF‚ą•CE and AF=CE. EF bisects AC (diagonals of parallelogram bisect each other). ‚úÖ

Q6. Show that line segments joining midpoints of opposite sides of a quadrilateral bisect each other.
Answer: Let P,Q,R,S be midpoints of AB,BC,CD,DA. Join AC. PR and QS are the segments joining midpoints of opposite sides. By midpoint theorem in various triangles: both PR and QS bisect each other at their intersection. ‚úÖ

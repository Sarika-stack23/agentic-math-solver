Class 8 | Ch13: Direct and Inverse Proportion
DIRECT PROPORTION:
x and y are in direct proportion if x/y=k (constant) or x1/y1=x2/y2.
As x increases, y increases proportionally. Symbol: x‚àùy.
Graph: straight line through origin.
Examples: cost‚àùquantity, distance‚àùtime(constant speed), simple interest‚àùtime.

Graph: hyperbola (not straight line).
Examples: speed‚àù1/time, workers‚àù1/days, pipes‚àù1/fill time.

8√ó12=6√ód ‚Üí d=96/6=16 days. (fewer workers ‚Üí more days ‚Üí inverse)
Example 3 (Inverse): 3 pipes fill tank in 8 hours. How long for 4 pipes?
3√ó8=4√ót ‚Üí t=24/4=6 hours.
Example 4: Car travels 150km in 3 hours. How long for 250km at same speed?
3/150=t/250 ‚Üí t=500/100=5 hours. (direct proportion)
IDENTIFYING TYPE:
More‚ÜíMore: Direct proportion.
More‚ÜíLess: Inverse proportion.
Workers and days: MORE workers, LESS days ‚Üí INVERSE.
Speed and time: MORE speed, LESS time ‚Üí INVERSE.
COMMON MISTAKES:

In inverse proportion: x1√óy1=x2√óy2 (NOT x1/y1=x2/y2).
